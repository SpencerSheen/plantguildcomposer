  1. Welcome to the Plant Guild Composer!

    You're a resident of Orange County, CA looking to transform your front yard into an edible landscape.

  2. Based on your food preference and site details the following plants are candidates for your yard.

    They key species are those that produce the bulk of your food. The support species can also do this but are there to ensure your key species is in good health.

  3. Click on a plant to view its characteristics, needs, and products.

  1. By looking at the needs of a key species and the products of a support species you can make good plant companionships.

  2. By taking weather data into consideration you can determine which plants are most suitable for your site. Scroll down to view annual temperature, precipitation and wind data.

  1. This is a map of your yard. You will be designing your guild on this map. Select a key species of your choice and click on the map to place it.

  1. Here you can see an inventory of which plants are in your guild.

  2. Now its time to chose a support species that provides your key species with the appropriate nutrients or pest control. Select a support species and place it on the map.

  1. Here you can see the viability of the guild. It is critical the the key and support species have enough nutrients and aren't bombarded by pests for the guild to be successful. Plants like Pigeon Pea, Mexican Sunflower, and Comfrey provide the guild with nutrients. Plants like Lemongrass, Chives, and Lavender provide the guild with pest control.

    Its important to realize that the larger your guild the more of these support species you'll require. For example, a guild with two avocados requires more nutrients and pest resistance than a guild with one!

  2. Keep arranging the plants in your yard until you have completed your design!

  1. Congratulations, you've formed a viable guild!

    This demo is over, but you are free to continue modifying your guild. Thanks for participating in the effort to make sustainable landscapes and food systems!
